Background (CCF BDCI)

The CCF Big Data & Computing Intelligence Contest (CCF BDCI) was founded in 2013 by the Chinese Computer Federation. It is a large-scale challenge series in data and AI, oriented towards real industry scenarios and frontiers of technology and application.

Over twelve editions, the contest has released 190+ real-world problems, engaging 200k+ participants from 1,500+ universities and 1,800+ organizations worldwide, with nearly 300k submissions. With its features of realistic data, global participation, training-through-competition, and in-depth themed exploration, CCF BDCI has become a marquee competition in big data and AI.

In 2025 (13th BDCI), the contest continues to provide a professional stage for practitioners to challenge themselves, accelerate solutions to real industry problems, and create a smarter future through data and intelligence.

Competition Background

Challenges

AI systems face pressing challenges in reliability, interpretability, and traceability. While deep learning is powerful, it lacks formal guarantees required by safety-critical domains.

Formal methods provide rigorous mathematical frameworks, yet traditional tools need higher automation and usability.

Paradigm Fusion

We explore the synergy of AI × Formal Methods:

  • Use AI to boost automation in theorem proving
  • Use formal methods to strengthen AI trustworthiness
  • Build verifiable pipelines for scientific discovery
  • Advance program synthesis and verification

Goals

Technical Breakthroughs

Advance ATP, scientific discovery agents, symbolic reasoning, and program verification with reproducible research.

Ecosystem Building

Unite global researchers and engineers to develop open datasets, baselines, and evaluation standards.

Talent Development

Foster talents with formal thinking and AI engineering skills for the next generation of trustworthy AI.
