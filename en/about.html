Competition Background (CCF BDCI)

The CCF Big Data & Computing Intelligence Contest (CCF BDCI) was established by the China Computer Federation in 2013 as a major challenge event in the fields of big data and artificial intelligence for algorithms, applications, and systems. The competition solicits requirements from key industries and application areas, focusing on cutting-edge technologies and practical industry problems, aiming to promote industry development and industrial upgrading. Through crowdsourcing and collective intelligence, it brings together wisdom from industry, academia, research, and application both domestically and internationally, discovering and nurturing a large number of high-quality data talents for society.

The competition has been successfully held for twelve editions to date, releasing over 190 challenge problems based on real industrial scenarios, attracting more than 200,000 participants from over 1,500 universities and 1,800 enterprises and institutions worldwide, with nearly 300,000 submissions of solutions and algorithmic works. With its distinctive competition format featuring "real data scenario problems, global cross-sector competition, learning through competition, and in-depth exploration of specialized topics," CCF BDCI has become a major challenge event in the fields of big data and artificial intelligence for algorithms, applications, systems, and entrepreneurship.

In 2025, we welcome the 13th CCF BDCI. The competition will continue to partner with industry elites, providing a professional platform for practitioners in big data and artificial intelligence nationwide to challenge themselves and hone their skills, accelerating the resolution of real industrial problems through competitive challenges, and creating a new future with data and intelligence.

Competition Background

Current Challenges

Current AI systems face significant challenges in reliability, interpretability, and traceability. While deep learning models are powerful, they lack formal guarantees and are difficult to apply in safety-critical domains.

Formal methods provide rigorous mathematical frameworks, but traditional tools need improvements in automation and usability.

Paradigm Integration

This competition is dedicated to exploring the synergistic path of AI √ó Formal Methods:

  • Using formal methods to strengthen AI trustworthiness
  • Building verifiable scientific discovery processes
  • Advancing program synthesis and verification technologies
